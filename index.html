Featured Essay

The Cartography of Leaving

"Every departure is a kind of draftsmanship — you are drawing boundaries around the life you're about to lose. I traced the edges of that apartment with my fingers the night before I left, the way a child traces a map they cannot yet read, following rivers that lead only to the margin of the page."

The Paris Review — Issue 248 — Spring 2025
Fig. 1 — The author's study, Brooklyn, 2024
Writing is the act of making the invisible visible — not by illumination, but by tracing the shape of what is absent.
— From "Fieldwork in Forgetting," Guernica, 2021
